Personal Statement:

Dr. Wang trained as a theoretical and computational physicist (2000) at Bergen University.
His first post-doc (2003) was at OUS - Norwegian Radium Hospital, to develop data-mining software for biologists for analyzing microarray experiments.
His second post-doc (2007) was at the Biology Department of Columbia University in New York City, to expand the knowledge in molecular biology and to be able to solve biology problems with computer simulation.
From year 2007 to now, he was a computational biologist or bioinformatics scientist, the main research interest is to design/develop advanced data mining and machine learning methods for solving problems in biology.



Group Aim:

The research aim is to have a better understanding of genome regulation, by developing advanced data mining and machine learning methods to explore big data generated by various experimental measurements.
In the past 10 years, our research has been devoted to the identification of functional regulatory mutations in disease and cancer, by integrating diverse information at gene promoter (e.g., genome, epigenome, transcriptome, 3D chromatin structure from bulk-cell sequencing).
In future, this research will be extended to the long-distance regulatory regions with new techniques (e.g., enhancer and single-cell sequencing), which may identify potential therapeutic targets. Additionally, we are interested in the development of user-friendly genomic data analysis packages for biologists, who wish to explore the molecular biology data by themselves.



Projects:

• To identify functional regulatory mutations in disease or cancer, by combining a biophysical model of TF-DNA interactions with diverse molecular experiments: Bayesian method for protein–DNA interaction with binding affinity ranking (BayesPI-BAR).

• To have a better understanding of long-distance gene regulation, by developing new data mining methods to explore complicated data structure from diverse sources (e.g., 3D chromatin structure, transcriptome, and epigenome analysis).

• To tackle various challenges in integrated data analysis of high throughput sequencing datasets (e.g., single cell sequencing, CUT&TAG, WGS, WGBS, and Hi-C et al.), by using advanced data mining and machine learning methods.
